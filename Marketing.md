# Marketing Notes

## ICP Review: What's Missing on successbycs.com

If the ICP is a founder, CRO, or CS leader at a growth-stage SaaS or MSP who wants help with retention, onboarding, renewals, and fractional CS leadership, the main thing missing is focus.

The site currently speaks to business owners and founders, SaaS founders, MSPs, growth-stage teams, APAC and global buyers, and also promotes workshops like incident and problem management. That breadth makes the offer feel broader than a single sharp ICP page.

### Main Gaps

1. A single primary ICP and promise above the fold

The current messaging mixes fractional CS leadership, onboarding and activation, renewals, RevOps metrics, client management, workshops, and incident/problem management. For an ICP-driven site, the homepage should lead with one dominant promise such as:

`We help ANZ/APAC SaaS founders lift retention and onboarding without hiring a full-time Head of CS.`

2. One clear conversion path

The main site currently pushes a collaboration session, PDF downloads, and multiple service angles. The funnel would be stronger if there were one primary CTA path tied to the core ICP and their most urgent problem.

3. More proof tied to the ICP

The site makes strong claims around churn reduction, NRR improvement, onboarding performance, and customer outcomes. What is missing is sharper founder-relevant proof:

- Named or semi-named case studies
- Before-and-after metrics
- Customer type or segment
- ARR band or company stage
- Tooling or operating context

4. Clearer "what happens in the first 2 weeks"

The offer would feel stronger with a more concrete onboarding motion. A high-converting ICP page usually makes the first engagement phase very tangible:

- What is audited first
- What gets fixed first
- What deliverables are produced
- What the buyer can expect to see in week 1 and week 2

5. Message consistency across the site

The broader site experience feels more like a general consulting/services business, while some narrower offer paths feel much more focused on retention and diagnostics. Those experiences should reinforce the same buyer, the same pain, and the same next step.

## Blunt Take

The site looks like it knows the work, but not yet the one buyer.

The fastest improvement would be to choose:

- One homepage ICP
- One flagship offer
- One proof story
- One primary CTA path

## Suggested Next Discussion

1. Define the best-fit ICP for the homepage
2. Rewrite the hero, subhead, and CTA for that ICP
3. Decide which sections should be removed, merged, or moved into secondary pages
4. Identify the best case study or proof asset to feature prominently
